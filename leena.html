New Hopes, 2019

Medium: Oil paint on canvas

Size: 16 x 24

Life is like a rollercoaster. There are always ups and downs. Even if a bad thing happens, I always want to see the good side of everything and believe that everything happens for a reason. I wanted to share this hope with others who needed it. I always thought the patterns of the marbles in my room were interesting so I decided to show this meaning in an art piece using them. In this piece, the colorful umbrella and marbles in the front and the broken umbrella with dark marbles in the back represent bad things that happen, but there’s always hope in the foreground when you take the time to look. I submitted it to the Congressional Art Competition so I could share the story with more people. Luckily, I placed second and got to share the story with Congresswoman Madeleine Dean and other audiences at the award ceremony. Currently, this art piece is displayed at a Congressional office in Washington D.C.

Unlimited, 2020

Medium: Oil paint on canvas

Size: 30 x 40

Time is limited. You can use it in any way you want whether you want to waste it or you can use it to make the most out of your life. For this piece, I got inspired by the marbles in my room because of their pattern. I wanted to share the meaning of "unlimited-ness" of life. Even though time is limited, there are unlimited ways to use it. I decided to use symbolism to represent that. First, I put the galaxy. Then, to connect marbles and galaxy, the marbles are turning into solar systems that follow into the broken clock. I personally think that it’s a very fun piece since it directs the audience' eyes into so many different ways. The repetition of the circle also makes this piece more entertaining.

Mom's Spaghetti, 2018

Medium: Acrylic on canvas

Size: 24 x 30

This painting is from my perspective as a teenage girl. I know that my mom is trying to look out for me and only wants what is best, such as good food. However, this puts a lot of pressure on me and causes stress. In this image, my mother has created a delicious and healthy meal for me but has not realized that in doing so, she has trapped me inside of the container that is constantly being shaken. I’ve focused on the composition and volume in this art piece so it’s more entertaining to the audience. I got first place at Upper Dublin School District Allied Art Show and honorable mention at a New York Korea Times Art Contest.

Childhood Memory, 2019

Medium: mixed media (colored pencil, watercolor, styrofoam board to create the box) Included real objects to photograph

This is one of my favorite pieces. The best childhood memory that I have is every Christmas, spending time with my family and exchanging gifts. I first drew the bow to study reflective objects and made the Christmas themed box using a styrofoam board. Then, I wanted to add meaning to this piece so I drew my future self reflecting on an ornament to show my old self missing childhood. To get a perfect photograph while showing the meaning, I've created the inside portion of the box and put real toys and objects from my childhood. I think I love this piece because it's very personal and it represents me well.

Louie
