Gulliver, 2018

Medium: Watercolor and Colored Pencil

Size: 24 x 19

Insecurity is something that all teenagers struggle with. It is a problem that strikes me especially though, tending to bring out a type of fear in myself that makes me feel smaller than everyone else around me. It is titled Gulliver because my own self-doubts makes me feel like I am in a world where I am only a small figure, surrounded by giants. I wanted to portray the struggle that I feel, struggling to match up with others; incomparable and smaller than everyone else.

Looking Upon, 2019

Medium: Graphite and Poster Color

Size: 30 x 40

In this piece, I tried to put emphasis on the holes. I wanted to embody the void that I feel inside, the flaws and insecurities that I hide deep inside by putting multiple rings, as if it were 3D. There are many overlapping each other and of different shades, making movement that are gravitating towards the right. I put the starkest hole within the image of myself, that was gazing to the right, as if looking at the holes from afar.

Searching, 2019

Medium: Poster Color and Ink

Size: 13 x 20

In this piece, it is me in the process of finding a way out - of finding my own path to take. Many of the hardest decisions that we make are during the time of a highschool senior. There are some choices that are hidden from us, others that seem like a mistake, and just the act of looking for the right way out seems like a trial. I tried to portray the franticness of myself through multiple depictions with an air of desperateness and frustration.

Hidden, 2018

Medium: Graphite

Size: 30 x 22

In response to fear, some people run, some people fight, and some people hide away from the world. I wanted to show how I am inclined to react to my own stifling insecurities and my tendency to lock myself so that no one can see. The lock and key that I submit myself to in the piece represents my isolation from society in response to my insecurity.
